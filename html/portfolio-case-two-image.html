Relative Pose Based Redundancy Removal: Collaborative RGB-D Data Transmission in a Mobile Visual Sensor Network

Description

We develop Relative Pose based Redundancy Removal (RPRR) scheme for bandwidth constrained operational scenarios. In our scheme, participating VSN nodes detect and remove the redundant visual and depth information leading to a significant improvement in wireless channel usage efficiency. Conventional approaches employ image comparison algorithms to determine the disparity among images and require at least one sensor to have the full knowledge of images captured by the others. These approaches are inevitably centralized, and are not able to avoid the transmission of redundant information before its removal at each sensor. In contrast, in the RPRR scheme, the nodes determine their relative pose and by using this knowledge they are able to distinguish the uncorrelated color and depth information from the rest locally. Consequently, this mechanism, which operates in a distributed manner, enables the nodes to transmit only non-redundant information.
